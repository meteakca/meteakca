abstract: Interest influences adults’ and young learners’ learning in formal and informal
  contexts. Although interest and interest development frameworks have been used in
  research on student-learning, they are not used in teacher-focused research, especially
  as “outcomes” of teacher professional development (PD) activities. In this study,
  we used interest development as the outcome of PD in computer science (CS) and investigated
  the factors that influenced teachers’ (n = 5) interest development toward CS using
  various data sources and analysis methods. We found that interest development is
  (a) varied, (b) influenced by self-relation, knowledge, and affect, (c) associated
  with reengagement with PD activities, and (d) it can be captured using computational
  text analysis methods and online log data.
